Responsive Web Design

What is Responsive Design?

With the adoption of devices such as smartphones and tablets, there are more people connected to the Internet at any given time. As such, there is increasing demand to ensure that websites render effectively from any device.

Responsive Web Design (RWD) is the practise of making content flexible and adjustable to accommodate different screen sizes, whether it be a smartphone, tablet or desktop.

What is Mobile First?

Mobile first is the practise of developing webpages starting with a smartphone layout and then developing layouts for larger devices such as a tablet or PC. This design process is important as people increasingly using their smartphones to access the internet.

What are Frameworks?

Frameworks are used to assist developers with responsive design. They contain pre-written code for HTML and CSS and are used to develop websites very quickly.

Pros include:

  1. Cross-Browser Functionality
  2. Speedy Development
  3. Consistent Code
  4. Clean and Symmetrical Layouts

Cons include:

  1. Extra Code
  2. Lack of Freedom
  3. Forced Semantic Changes

What are Wireframes?

Wireframes are part of the design phase and are used to determine the basic layout of a page. They are used to visually represent what a page could look like and are helpful to represent major elements during the design phase. For responsive sites, you may need to do wireframes for multiple devices.

Why are wireframes important?

  1. They establish a hierarchy of information on a page
  2. Simplifies communication between you and your client
  3. Acts as a visual blueprint when you get to the design phase

The most challenging aspect of wireframes i found was that it was quite easy to spend too much time doing it. Timeboxing is key!
