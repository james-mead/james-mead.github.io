Daily Mindfulness

This week at EDA we were asked to read chapter 3 of Search Inside Yourself. Here are my learnings:

Mindful Conversation

There are three core components to mindful conversation:

  1. Mindful Listening - whereby you listen to someone speak and fully devoting your attention, uninterrupted for three minutes.
  2. Looping - whereby you 'loop' back to the speaker and provide feedback to confirm your understanding of what they have just said.
  3. Dipping - whereby you check in with yourself to minimize distractions. You can think of dipping as self-directed mindfulness during listening.

I found the 'Dipping' technique to be quite useful. It's important to gently bring back focus to the person speaking during conversation.

One sentence that really stood out to me was the fact that relaxation is the foundation for deep concentration. Essentially, when the mind is relaxed, it becomes more calm and stable, and when the mind is calm and stable, it becomes relaxed.
