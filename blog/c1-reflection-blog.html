Reflection

The way people learn is changing. EDA offers a new approach to learning which is fully immersive, iterative, high paced and puts learning in the hands of the individual. The course not only teaches technical skills, but helps develop a lot of soft skills which are a necessity in order to become a great developer.

First impressions of this course tell me that you only get out what you put in and it really depends on the individual as to how well one succeeds. I like the agile approach to learning and the fact that each week is organised into sprints, just like a dev team in a workplace environment. I also like the fully immersive learning style as i find this the best way to learn. I was a little surprised to know that Dev Academy's course curriculum differs from Dev Boot Camp's, however i see this as a positive.

It's encouraging to see the amount of passion and excitement from not only the teachers but the students who are currently studying at Dev Academy.

The culture is a little new to me. Although I traditionally work at pace and take a more agile approach to development and implementation, I have historically worked in isolation. This is due to the nature of work I have worked in. I'm exited to work as part of a team again and to see what strengths each person brings.

It's always both exciting and nerve-wracking trying something new. I am changing careers and I will also be changing location at the end of this course, so that will make things a little challenging.
