What's your take on the DBC/EDA experience?

The way people learn is changing. EDA offers a new approach to learning which is fully immersive, iterative, high paced and puts learning in the hands of the individual. The course not only teaches technical skills, but helps develop a lot of soft skills which are a necessity in order to become a great developer.

What are your impressions?

It's encouraging to see the amount of passion and excitement from not only the teachers but the students who are currently doing or have recently completed bootcamp.

First impressions of this course tell me that you only get out what you put in and it really depends on individual as to how well one succeeds. I like the agile approach to learning and the fact that each week is organised into sprints, just like a dev team. I also like the fully immersive learning style as i find this the best way to learn.

How do you see yourself engaging with this type of culture?

This will be a little new to me. Although I traditionally work at pace and take a more agile approach to development and implementation, I have historically worked in isolation. This is due to the nature of work I have worked in. I'm exited to work as part of a team again and to see what strengths each person brings.

Have your expectations of EDA changed? If so, how?

I was a little surprise to know that EDA's course curriculum differs from DBA. For example, DBA teaches Ruby, whereas EDA teaches Javascript.

Are you excited to participate in this kind of learning environment? Does it make you nervous?

It's always both exciting and nerve-wracking trying something new. I am changing careers and will also be changing location at the end of this course, so that will make things a little challenging.
